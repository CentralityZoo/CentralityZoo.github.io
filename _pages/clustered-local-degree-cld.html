--- title: Clustered local-degree (CLD) permalink: /clustered-local-degree-cld/ search: true ---

The clustered local-degree (CLD) centrality integrates both the degree of neighboring nodes and the local topological structure surrounding each node. In this measure, the local clustering coefficient of a node quantifies the connectivity among its neighbors [2]. The centrality value \( c_{\mathrm{CLD}}(i) \) for node \( i \) is defined as\begin{equation*}c_{\mathrm{CLD}}(i) = (1 + c_i) \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} d_j,\end{equation*}where \( c_i \) denotes the clustering coefficient of node \( i \), \( \mathcal{N}(i) \) is the set of neighbors of node \( i \), and \( d_j \) represents the degree of neighbor \( j \). Hence, the CLD centrality accounts for both the number of neighbors and the connectivity among them.
