--- title: Stress centrality permalink: /stress-centrality/ search: true ---

Stress centrality quantifies the amount of "stress" a node experiences due to the network's activity, quantified by how often the node lies on the shortest paths connecting pairs of other nodes [2]. For a node \(i\), the stress centrality, denoted by \(c_{Stress}(i)\), is defined as the total number of shortest paths between all pairs of nodes that pass through \(i\):\begin{equation*}c_{Stress}(i) = \sum_{j \neq k \neq i} σ_{jk}(i),\end{equation*}where \(σ_{jk}(i)\) denotes the number of shortest paths from node \(j\) to node \(k\) that pass through node \(i\).
