Length-scaled betweenness centrality (LSBC)


Length-scaled betweenness centrality (LSBC), also known as distance-scaled betweenness, is a variant of betweenness centrality that weights shortest paths inversely proportional to their length [2, 3]. The centrality of node \(i\) is defined as
\begin{equation*}
c_{b_{dist}}(i) = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \sum_{k=1}^{N} \frac{1}{d_{jk}} \frac{ﾏダ{jk}(i)}{ﾏダ{jk}},
\end{equation*}
where \(d_{jk}\) is the length of the shortest path from \(j\) to \(k\), \(ﾏダ{jk}\) is the total number of shortest paths between \(j\) and \(k\), and \(ﾏダ{jk}(i)\) is the number of those paths passing through \(i\). This measure reflects the intuition that longer paths contribute less to a node's centrality.
