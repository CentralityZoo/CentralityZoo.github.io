--- title: Borgatti's effective size permalink: /borgatti-s-effective-size/ search: true ---

Borgatti’s effective size , introduced by [2], is a simplified reformulation of Burt’s effective size measure [3]. It defines the effective size \( c_{\mathrm{BES}}(i) \) of ego \( i \)’s network as\begin{equation*}c_{\mathrm{BES}}(i) = d_i - c_{r}(i),\end{equation*}where \( d_i \) denotes the degree of node \( i \), and \( c_{r}(i) \) represents the redundancy measure also proposed by [2]. This formulation expresses the number of non-redundant contacts in ego \( i \)’s network, emphasizing that relationships linking \( i \) to otherwise unconnected individuals provide access to diverse and independent sources of information or resources.
