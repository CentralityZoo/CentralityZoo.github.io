--- title: Mapping entropy (ME) centrality permalink: /mapping-entropy-me-centrality/ search: true ---

Mapping entropy (ME) is a semi-local measure that incorporates both the degree of a node and the degrees of its neighbors [2, 3]. The ME of node \(i\) is defined as\[c_{\mathrm{ME}}(i) = - d_i \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} \log d_j,\]where \(d_i\) is the degree of node \(i\) and \(\mathcal{N}(i)\) denotes the set of neighbors of node \(i\). ME emphasizes nodes with high degree that are connected to neighbors with a wide range of degrees, thus combining node-level and neighborhood-level information.
