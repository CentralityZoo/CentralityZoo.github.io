--- title: Global Structure Model (GSM) permalink: /global-structure-model-gsm/ search: true ---

The Global Structure Model (GSM) centrality accounts for self-influence (SI) and global influence (GI) [2]. For node \(i\), the GSM score is defined as\[c_{GSM}(i) = SI(i) \cdot GI(i) = e^{k_s(i)/N} \cdot \sum_{j \neq i} \frac{k_s(j)}{d_{ij}},\]where \(k_s(i)\) is the \(k\)-shell value of \(i\), \(d_{ij}\) is the shortest-path distance between \(i\) and \(j\) and \(N\) is the total number of nodes in the network. The term \(SI(i)\) reflects intrinsic influence based on the core-periphery position, while \(GI(i)\) captures contributions from all other nodes weighted by distance.
