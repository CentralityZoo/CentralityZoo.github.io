--- title: Weighted gravity model (WGravity) permalink: /weighted-gravity-model-wgravity/ search: true ---

The weighted gravity model (WGravity) is a variant of the traditional gravity model that incorporates a truncation radius and the eigenvector centrality of nodes [2]. The WGravity centrality of node \(i\) is defined as\[c_{WGravity}(i) = e_i \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}^{(\leq r)}(i)} \frac{d_i d_j}{d_{ij}^2},\]where \(d_i\) and \(d_j\) are the degrees of nodes \(i\) and \(j\), \(d_{ij}\) is the shortest distance between nodes \(i\) and \(j\), \(e_i\) is the eigenvector centrality of node \(i\), and \(\mathcal{N}^{(\leq r)}(i)\) denotes the set of nodes within \(r\) hops of \(i\). The parameter \(r\) defines the radius of influence for each node. Liu et al. [2] suggest setting \(r = 0.5 \langle d \rangle\), where \(\langle d \rangle\) is the average shortest path length of the network \(G\). This truncation balances local and semi-local information while incorporating the global influence captured by eigenvector centrality.
