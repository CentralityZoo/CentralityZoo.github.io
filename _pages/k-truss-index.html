The k -truss index -truss index} evaluates the influence of a node based on the \(k\)-truss decomposition of a graph \(G\) [2]. A \(k\)-truss is the maximal subgraph in which every edge participates in at least \(k-2\) triangles. Equivalently, a \(k\)-clique corresponds to a \((k-2)\)-truss and a \(k\)-truss is contained within a \((k+1)\)-core. Malliaros et al. [3] demonstrate that nodes belonging to the maximal \(k\)-truss subgraph exhibit stronger spreading influence than nodes identified by other centrality measures, including degree and \(k\)-core index.
